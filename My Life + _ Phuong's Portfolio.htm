Mylife+

Supporting you to take control of your health, happiness, and connectedness with an easy to use health and wellbeing app for people with HIV.

The Problem:

In 2015, it was estimated that there were 25,313 people living with HIV in Australia. ’Living with HIV’ is a term used to describe a positive diagnosis: when a HIV positive person is on treatment, they can live as long and as healthy a life as a person who is HIV negative. However, they are at risk with additional health conditions and having regular checks with GPs can give them early warnings about changes in health and effectiveness of their treatment. 

“There are a lot of closeted people living with HIV, they get diagnosed, go see their doctor and they don’t tell anyone else.”

-Craig Burnett, HIV advocate

The Approach:

Living with HIV isn’t only about the negative health issue and difficulty in managing one’s life but also the stigma, it can isolate the patient from the positive community. My aim to address this problem is to steer away from the clinical look of cold hard data and present them in a more lifestyle look. Charts and graphs can still be ‘sexy’ and informative. 

“As someone living with HIV today, I’m confident that I can live into old age with my condition, but I’m also aware that I need to take care of my health and remain consciously aware of how my health is progressing, that’s really important.”

-Robert Mitchell, Vice-President of NAPWHA-

 

The Branding

The app has been launched by ViiV Healthcare in collaboration with the National Association of People with HIV Australia (NAPWHA). As part of the national MyLife+ promotional campaign, our team at Marcel Sydney has created a branding that is influenced from the app design.
